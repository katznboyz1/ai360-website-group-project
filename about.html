AI360 is a year-long initiative that aims to close the employment gap for people with disabilities
by educating companies and connecting them to talented job seekers. The initiative creates a forum for employers to share
their D&I strengths and challenges, particularly in the area of recruiting and retaining people with intellectual and
developmental disabilities (I/DD). It also opens the conversation for job seekers with disabilities, who aren't always offered
a fair opportunity to access competitive jobs.

HeartShare Human Services of New York launched the AI360 initiative and is made
possible by its founding sponsor The FAR Fund, which provides funding to programs that promote healthy social and emotional
development in children and families. HeartShare Human Services of New York nurtures and empowers children and adults diagnosed
with I/DD, including autism spectrum disorders, cerebral palsy and Down Syndrome. From residential and education to health and
respite services, HeartShare offers greater access to a more independent life. HeartShare has a growing employment program, which
trains and places candidates in competitive positions in corporate offices, community organizations, higher education and beyond.
